# 22/06/2026-26/06/2026

I’m very excited to be back for another summer internship with [TESSERA](https://geotessera.org/), the skills I learned last summer were incredibly helpful on my courses at university and really helped me understand the possible applications of the algorithms and methods I was learning.
This summer I am going to be working on hedgehog conservation after some of the other work done by researchers using TESSERA got significant attention, including coverage like this [BBC article](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c202g60qrlpo)

I spent the start of the week familiarising myself with the progress and development of TESSERA in the last 9 months whilst I’ve been away. This involved creating new tiles with the v1.1 embeddings to compare them to my work from last summer. It was great to see that even with a lack of post processing, something that was very necessary last year, TESSERA has improved it's predictive ability significantly and solved a lot of inconsistencies.

Additionally, I had an enjoyable few hours learning more about hedgehogs to give me some background knowledge on their habits, lifestyle, habitat etc.

Towards the end of the week I began my first proper task, examining the *Farmscapes 2020 Vectorised* dataset from Google to see if I could use their data to train TESSERA to also detect hedgerows: something that is tricky due to their narrow profile. Hopefully with a dataset large enough (in this case the size of the UK) there will be enough training points to result in TESSERA being able to detect the location of these small features reliably.
